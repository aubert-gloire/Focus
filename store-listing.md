## Full Description

**FOCUS keeps you on task — automatically.**

Tell FOCUS what you're working on, how long you plan to work, and paste the link to where you'll be. That's it. FOCUS does the rest.

**How it works**

When you switch to an unrelated tab — say Instagram while you're supposed to be on Coursera — FOCUS intercepts it immediately and shows you a 10-second countdown. It reminds you of your task and sends you straight back to where you were. You can override if it was intentional, and FOCUS won't flag that site again for the rest of your session.

**Designed for real study and work scenarios**

- Watching a Python tutorial on YouTube? Paste the video link. FOCUS allows YouTube but blocks Instagram, Twitter, TikTok, and anything unrelated to your task.
- Doing readings on Perusall? Paste your assignment link. FOCUS keeps you there and allows legitimate research (Wikipedia, Google Scholar) while blocking distractions.
- Taking a Coursera course? Paste your course URL. FOCUS lets you navigate freely within Coursera and blocks everything off-task.
- Debugging code on GitHub? FOCUS allows Stack Overflow, documentation, and PyPI — because those are part of the job.

**Powered by on-device machine learning**

FOCUS uses a lightweight classifier that runs entirely in your browser to decide whether a tab is related to your task. No cloud calls. No data leaving your device. The model considers keyword overlap between your task description and each tab you visit, so it gets smarter with context — not just a blocklist.

**Privacy first**

Everything stays on your device. FOCUS has no servers, no accounts, and no analytics. Your browsing data is never transmitted anywhere. When you close your browser, the session is cleared completely — fresh start every time.

**Features**
- Set task, expected duration, and task link in under 10 seconds
- Instant tab blocking with 10-second countdown and override option
- Session duration alert when your time is up
- Real-time focus score and blocks-fired counter in the popup
- Works on Chrome and Firefox
